Leading barristers Jeremy Dein and Sasha Wass look back at their investigation into whether a young mother accused of poisoning her family in 1850 was hanged as a result of hearsay and gossip. In the mid-19th century, a poison panic gripped Victorian Britain when a slew of murders, many perpetrated by women, caused national hysteria. Their weapon of choice was an easily available powdered poison called arsenic.
