The flood of funds into sculptural projects during the burgeoning British Empire unleashed a new, yet remarkably unorthodox, golden age. The greatest sculptors of the 18th and 19th centuries were mavericks who bucked prevailing trends - geniuses like John Flaxman, Francis Chantrey and Alfred Gilbert. Sooke reveals the technical breakthroughs behind their key works.
