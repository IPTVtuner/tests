Filmed during lockdown, Alan Yentob invites us into the intriguing world of award-winning novelist Kazuo Ishiguro. The only living British author to hold the Nobel Prize in Literature, Ishiguro's novels and short stories have been translated into more than fifty languages. Two of his most popular novels, `The Remains of the Day' and `Never Let Me Go', have also enjoyed success as star-studded film adaptations. In this revealing profile, Ishiguro explores the significance of his early life.
