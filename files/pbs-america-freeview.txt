There have been many false dawns in the conflict, but finally the desire for change propels the possibility of peace for the next generation. Following years of talks at a government level, paramilitary ceasefires are announced on both sides. And when the Good Friday Agreement is finalised in April 1998, there is optimism about new beginnings. But conflict has exacted a heavy cost, and its effects on victims and survivors are lasting and significant.
