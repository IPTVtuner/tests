Writers and historians give their own interpretations on the last days of Anne Boleyn. In 1536 she became the first queen in Britain's history to be executed. The brutal speed of her downfall and the nature of the charges against her retain their power to shock. We know how Anne died, but the story of why she had to go and who authored her violent end has been the subject of fiery debate across six centuries.
