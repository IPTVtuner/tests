In December 1989, Navy Seal divers brave enemy waters to destroy Panamanian dictator Manuel Noriega's heavily guarded gunboat and prevent his escape. The Navy Seals use a number of evasive tactics to approach surreptitiously since the entire US invasion of Panama could be compromised if they are spotted. However, when the invasion is brought forward by 15 minutes, the divers cannot be contacted because their underwater radios are not working.
