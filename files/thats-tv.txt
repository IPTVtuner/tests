Germany's Hindenburg airship was as big as the Titanic, one of the great engineering feats and mechanical sights of the early 20th century. On Thursday 6 May 1937 disaster struck, when it caught fire in New Jersey. Now, in the most ambitious testing programme ever undertaken, a team of experts put the leading theories to the test in the field to establish which was most likely to have caused the crash.
