Stretching around 16 miles long and four miles wide, Strangford Lough is the largest tidal sea lough in both the UK and Ireland - it also has 70 islands and is one of Europe's most biodiverse regions. In this episode, Fred's cycling journey along the lough's scenic shores takes him to the 18th-century mansion of Castle Ward that is famous for its two contrasting architectural styles.
